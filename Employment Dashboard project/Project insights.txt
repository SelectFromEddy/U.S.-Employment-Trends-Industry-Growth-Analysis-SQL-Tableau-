# Employment Trends & Industry Insights (2024–2025)


## Which industries employ the most workers?

Health Care & Social Assistance was the largest employer in the dataset, accounting for approximately 21.0% of total employment. Professional & Business Services followed closely at 20.2%, meaning these two industries represented more than 40% of total employment.

Leisure & Hospitality accounted for 15.2% of employment, followed by Retail Trade (13.9%) and Manufacturing (11.4%). Together, these industries represented the majority of jobs in the dataset.

Financial Activities (8.3%), Construction (7.4%), and Information (2.6%) accounted for a smaller share of total employment.

---

## Which industries are growing or declining?

Health Care & Social Assistance recorded the strongest year-over-year employment growth at approximately +3.5%, making it the highest-growth industry in the dataset.

Construction (+0.7%), Leisure & Hospitality (+0.6%), and Financial Activities (+0.4%) also recorded positive employment growth.

Retail Trade experienced a slight decline (−0.3%), while Professional & Business Services (−0.7%) and Manufacturing (−1.2%) showed larger employment decreases.

Information recorded the largest year-over-year decline at approximately −2.1%, making it the weakest-performing industry during the period analyzed.

---

## Which industries are growing the fastest?

Health Care & Social Assistance demonstrated the strongest month-over-month growth, averaging approximately +0.25% per month**, making it the most consistent source of employment growth in the dataset.

Leisure & Hospitality and Financial Activities recorded smaller positive monthly gains, while Manufacturing, Professional & Business Services, and Information experienced slight monthly declines.

Overall, employment growth was concentrated in a limited number of industries rather than being broadly distributed across the labor market.

---

## What are the seasonal hiring patterns?

Using non-seasonally adjusted benchmark revised BLS data reveals noticeable seasonal employment patterns across several industries.

Construction generally showed stronger employment levels during spring and summer months and slower growth during colder months.

Retail Trade displayed seasonal increases toward the end of the year followed by declines after the holiday period.

Leisure & Hospitality also showed recurring seasonal fluctuations throughout the year.

In contrast, Health Care & Social Assistance exhibited relatively small seasonal changes compared with other industries.

---

## Which industries are stable or volatile?

Health Care & Social Assistance was the most stable industry in the dataset, showing relatively small month-to-month fluctuations alongside consistent long-term growth.

Financial Activities and Manufacturing also exhibited comparatively stable employment patterns.

Retail Trade and Leisure & Hospitality demonstrated moderate variability, while Construction showed greater fluctuations throughout the year.

Information was the most volatile industry, recording the largest employment variability and the steepest year-over-year decline (−2.1%).
